How to Bulk Delete Instagram Posts (Step-by-Step Guide)

Tired of your old aesthetic resurfacing? Want a clean Instagram grid before a rebrand, a job interview, or a fresh start? This guide walks through deleting every post, Reel, like, and follow on your account in bulk — safely, with filters, in about 15 minutes per 1,000 posts.

Why bulk delete Instagram posts?

Most people hit a point where they want a clean Instagram — a rebrand, a job hunt, a move to a new city, a fresh start, or just the realization that your grid is a museum of 2018 you. The problem: Instagram's official "Delete" workflow is one post at a time. There is no built-in bulk delete, no batch action, no hashtag filter, and no time-range cleanup.

For 200 posts, that's an afternoon of tapping. For 2,000 posts, it's a week. For 5,000 posts, it's effectively impossible without help.

That's what SocialEraser for Instagram is for. It runs in your browser, simulates normal clicks at human speed, and lets you filter by date, hashtag, engagement floor, or post type. You set the rules, click Start, and walk away. This guide walks through the whole process.

Before you start: back up your data

This is the most important step, and you should do it before your first run. Once you delete an Instagram post, it is gone — there is no recycle bin, no undo, no 30-day restore window for posts deleted via the API. The Instagram "Archive" feature does offer a 30-day restore window, but it requires using the in-app archive button.

The official way to get your posts back (even after deletion) is to request your Download your information archive from Instagram before you start. The archive contains every post, Reel, story, comment, like, and follower as standalone files.

  1. Open instagram.com in your browser, log in.
  2. Visit instagram.com/download/request directly.
  3. Choose JSON + HTML format, select All available information, then click Request download.
  4. Wait for Instagram to email you the download link. It usually takes a few hours, sometimes up to 48.

Tip: Even if you plan to delete everything, request this archive first. It's free, it's your data, and it gives you a way back if you ever change your mind.

Step-by-step: delete every Instagram post in bulk

The whole process takes about 60 seconds of setup. The actual deletion runs in the background.

1

Install SocialEraser for Instagram

Go to the SocialEraser for Instagram page and click Join Instagram Waitlist. The extension is currently in pre-launch — joining the waitlist gets you early access when it ships. (You can also install SocialEraser for X today, which is fully launched.)

2

Open instagram.com and log in

Navigate to instagram.com in the browser where you installed SocialEraser. Log in to your account as usual. The extension doesn't handle passwords — Instagram's own login does. SocialEraser only acts on the page after you're already authenticated.

Click the SocialEraser icon in your toolbar to open the side panel. The status indicator at the top will go from yellow to green once it confirms you're logged in.

3

Pick what to clean

In the side panel, tick the boxes for what you want to delete. You can pick one or combine them:

  • Posts — your own uploaded photos and carousels
  • Reels — every Reel in your profile
  • Likes — every post in your Likes tab
  • Saved Collections — your private saved posts
  • Following — accounts you follow
  • Comments — your own comments on other posts
  • Story Highlights — your archived Story Highlights
  • DMs — direct message threads

If you only want to clean one type (say, just your uploaded posts), tick only that one. The extension will skip the others entirely.

4

Set your filters (optional but recommended)

Filters are what separate a precise bulk delete from a nuke-everything rampage. The four filters are covered in detail in the next section, but the most common setup is:

  • From date: keep recent posts, delete everything before a specific date
  • Hashtag: only delete posts containing a specific hashtag
  • Engagement floor: keep posts above X likes, delete everything below
  • Post type: only photos, only carousels, or only Reels

You can combine multiple filters. For example, "delete all posts before Jan 1, 2022 with the #travel hashtag and fewer than 50 likes" is a single filter combination.

5

Click Start and let it run

Hit the pink-to-purple Start Cleanup button. The extension opens each matching item, clicks the delete / unlike / unfollow button at human speed (about 0.9 seconds per item), and updates a live progress counter.

You can:

  • Watch it work in real time on the side panel
  • Minimize the tab and use your browser normally
  • Close the laptop entirely — state persists across browser restarts, so when you come back, it resumes from the last completed item
  • Hit Pause at any time to take a break
  • Hit Stop if you change your mind mid-run

For a typical 1,000-post library, the full run takes about 15 minutes. For 5,000 posts, expect 1.5–2 hours of background time, with no babysitting required.

The four filters that make bulk delete precise

Filters turn a blunt instrument into a scalpel. Here are the four available in SocialEraser for Instagram, and the use cases each one solves.

Date range

Delete posts posted before or after any specific date. This is the most common filter. Use it to keep your recent work and clear out the old era. The "before" direction is also the right tool if you want a clean grid before a rebrand or a job interview.

Hashtag match

Only delete posts whose caption contains a specific hashtag. Great for clearing a single topic — like a brand deal, a heated take, a fandom era, or anything else you want gone without touching the rest of your library.

Engagement floor

Keep posts above a like threshold, delete the rest. Set 100 to keep your breakout moments. Set 0 to delete everything regardless of likes. This is the best filter for keeping your viral hits while clearing the lonely 0-like pile.

Post type

Filter to only photos, only carousels, or only Reels. Useful for keeping a specific content format while clearing others, or for backing up your best carousels before wiping the rest.

You can combine any of the four. The most popular combo is "before Jan 1, 2022 AND fewer than 50 likes" — keeps the breakout era, clears the experimental pile.

What gets deleted vs. what stays

SocialEraser only touches what you tell it to touch. Specifically:

  • Posts checkbox ON — your own uploaded photos and carousels get deleted one by one.
  • Reels checkbox ON — every Reel in your profile gets deleted.
  • Likes checkbox ON — every post in your Likes tab is unliked.
  • Saved Collections checkbox ON — every saved post in your collections is unsaved.
  • Following checkbox ON — every account you follow is unfollowed, in order. The order matters if you want to pick up where you left off; if not, the run is just as effective.
  • Comments checkbox ON — every comment you've posted gets deleted.
  • Story Highlights checkbox ON — every Highlight in your profile gets deleted.
  • DMs checkbox ON — every direct message thread gets deleted (one-sided; the recipient still has a copy).

What SocialEraser does not touch: your account settings, your drafts, your profile photo, your bio, your username, your highlight covers, your close friends list, or any other content on Instagram that you didn't explicitly check a box for.

Safety, rate limits, and account safety

SocialEraser simulates normal clicks at human speed — no API calls, no scraping, no headless browser, no mass automation. The extension literally clicks the same buttons you would click, one at a time, with a delay between each one. The average is about 0.9 seconds per item, with a randomized jitter so it doesn't look robotic.

That said, the same caveat applies as with any third-party tool. We are not affiliated with Meta Platforms, Inc. Using bulk tools is at your own discretion. If Instagram changes their UI, our remote-config update system pushes a new selector schema within hours — no extension reinstall needed.

If you hit a rate limit, the extension pauses automatically, shows a "reconnecting" indicator, and resumes when the limit lifts. The state is preserved across browser restarts, so a 5,000-post run can be split across multiple days with no duplication or skipped items.

One more time: request your Instagram data archive before your first bulk run. The official "Download your information" page is the only way to get your posts back after deletion.

Frequently asked questions

How long does it take to delete all my Instagram posts?

SocialEraser processes about 0.9 seconds per item. 1,000 posts take roughly 15 minutes. 5,000 posts take about 1.5–2 hours of background time. You can leave the run going while you sleep, work, or do anything else.

Can I delete only posts posted before a specific date?

Yes. Use the From date filter in the side panel. Posts posted on or after the date are kept; everything older is deleted. This is the most common filter — many people use it to clear out everything before a rebrand or a job interview.

Can I keep my most-liked posts and delete only the rest?

Yes. Use the Engagement floor filter and set a threshold. Posts above the threshold stay; posts below it get deleted. A common choice is 50 likes — keeps the breakout moments, clears the noise.

Does this work for deleting Reels, likes, and follows too?

Yes. SocialEraser can bulk-delete Reels, bulk-unlike posts, clear saved collections, and bulk-unfollow accounts. The same filter system (date, hashtag, engagement floor, post type) applies to all cleanup types. Tick whichever boxes match what you want gone.

Will my account get banned?

SocialEraser simulates normal clicks at human speed and respects Instagram's rate limits. The same caveat applies as with any third-party tool — we are not affiliated with Meta Platforms, Inc., and using bulk tools is at your own discretion.

Can I undo a delete after the fact?

No. Once a post is deleted, it is gone from Instagram's public view within seconds. This is why the first step of this guide is to back up your data first using Instagram's official archive. Note: posts moved to archive (instead of deleted) remain recoverable for 30 days from the Instagram app.

Does this work on the Instagram mobile app?

No. The Instagram mobile app does not allow browser extensions. SocialEraser runs in Chrome on desktop. A dedicated mobile companion is on the 2027 roadmap.

What if I have thousands of posts?

SocialEraser handles large libraries fine. The extension pauses automatically if it hits a rate limit, and you can resume from the same spot. State is preserved across browser restarts, so a 5,000-post library can be split across multiple days with no duplication or skipped items.
